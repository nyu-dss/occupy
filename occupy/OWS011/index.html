Description
The Occupier was one of many periodicals that was launched during Occupy Wall Street, and is an example of the various forms of grassroots media that circulated about and by OWS during the height of the movement. The Occupier was produced by Organizing for Occupation, O4O, who primarily focused on issues of housing rights, foreclosure, and evictions -- some of the chief concerns of Occupy Wall Street in response to the widespread foreclosures in the United States, primarily between 2007-2010, also known as the foreclosure crisis. The foreclosure crisis, also known as the subprime mortgage crisis, in which banks seized properties following years of predatory lending, was compounded by the Great Recession of 2008. Both of these events gave rise to the Occupy movement.
